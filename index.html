Introduction

Regulatory Compliance

Basel 3.1 demands precision at every level. PRISM brings AI-powered intelligence to a process still dominated by manual effort — reducing risk, accelerating timelines, and delivering audit-ready outputs.

The Regulatory Landscape

Financial institutions face an escalating compliance burden. The Bank of England's Annex II reporting instructions span 322 pages of formulas, rules, and cross-references — each requiring precise interpretation before any template can be populated.

The industry still relies on specialist teams manually reading, interpreting, and transcribing these rules into calculation engines. This process is slow, expensive, and inherently error-prone.

The core challenge: Translating 322 pages of dense regulatory language into executable, auditable calculations without introducing error or ambiguity.

What PRISM Changes

PRISM applies large language models and structured AI pipelines to automate the entire chain — from raw PDF ingestion to a fully populated, traceable COREP Excel workbook.

Every formula is extracted, validated, and executed programmatically. Every output cell carries a complete audit trail. Every run is reproducible — down to the bit.

The result: Compliance teams shift from manual transcription to intelligent oversight — reviewing outputs rather than producing them.

01
Document Intelligence Layer

The PDFium C-engine extracts clean text from all 322 pages. Six critical mathematical symbols are Unicode-normalised. Watermarks and footers stripped. The document is then segmented into 500-word overlapping chunks with metadata enrichment — detecting section headings, template codes, and regulatory references.

pypdfium2Unicode normalisationSmart chunkingFAISS vector index
02
Rule Extraction & Enrichment

A two-layer detection architecture: regex signal patterns identify formula candidates (explicit expressions, aggregations, min/max, multiplications). LLM enrichment then provides context-aware interpretation, maps rules to template codes (CA1, OF CR SA, CCR), assigns confidence scores, and produces structured FormulaRecord objects with legal references.

LLM interpretationRegex signal detectionFormulaRecord schemaConfidence scoring
03
Calculation Engine

SymPy evaluates every extracted formula with symbolic precision — no floating-point approximation. Row-level parameters (He, Hc, Hfx, t, T, t*, risk_weight) are applied per row. Hierarchical aggregation flows from leaf rows through subtotals to grand totals, mirroring the exact COREP template structure.

04
Output & Audit Generation

Two Excel workbooks produced: .xlsx and macro-enabled .xlsm. Colour coding distinguishes input cells (yellow) from calculated cells (blue). Every calculated cell carries a comment with formula ID, expression, step-by-step walkthrough, inputs, result, and ISO timestamp. A JSON audit log captures the complete calculation trail for automated reconciliation.
